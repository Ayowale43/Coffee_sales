## Sales and Customer Purchase Analysis Report

This report focuses on analyzing sales performance across urban and suburban regions, comparing weekday and weekend trends, as well as evaluating sales distribution across different periods of the day. In addition, the report examines customer-product purchase relationships to better understand customer loyalty and purchasing behavior.

The analysis revealed that urban areas generated a dominant 86% of total sales, significantly outperforming suburban areas, which contributed 14% of total sales. This highlights the stronger market concentration and customer activity within urban locations.

A time-based sales analysis showed relatively close performance between morning and afternoon sales periods. Morning sales accounted for 34% of total sales, while afternoon sales contributed 32%. Evening sales represented 22%, whereas night sales recorded the lowest contribution at 13%. These findings indicate that customer purchasing activity was highest during the earlier parts of the day.

Furthermore, customer-product purchase relationships were segmented into loyalty tiers: Loyal, Gold, Silver, Bronze, and None. The analysis showed that 8.4% of customers belonged to the Loyal tier, 30.6% to the Gold tier, 41.4% to the Silver tier, and 19.5% to the Bronze/None category. The results suggest that the majority of customers fall within the mid-level loyalty segments, presenting opportunities for customer retention and loyalty enhancement strategies.

The analysis and visualizations were developed using Power Query, Power BI, and PowerPoint.
